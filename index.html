Quality of Life Issue Reporter

As part of a Fellowship year with Code for America, my teammates and I built a toolkit for residents of Vallejo, CA to report quality of life issues about nuisance properties via phone call or webform. For city staff members handling those reports, we built a complementary tool to manage the workflow, enable collaboration across departments, and provide new insights. There's not currently a public-facing version of the tool, but you can view the repo here.

Where's The Bus Stop?

In 2014, King County Metro, which provides bus service in the Seattle Metro area, proposed eliminating and shortening certain routes as a cost-saving measure. I was curious which parts of the city would have been most affected by the proposed changes, so I calcultaed the increase in time spent walking to the nearest bus stop. You can find the visualization here.

NYPD Stop and Frisk

I joined demographic data from New York City Community Districts with stop and frisk data from the NYPD and created a visualization to help understand how socio-economic factors of neighborhoods are associated with police-civilian interactions in those neighborhoods. You can find the visualization here.

Adventure Time

This project is a simple exploration in transforming GEOTiff files and cutting them into map tiles. In this specific example, I used topographic maps from the Canadian Department of Natural Resources and plotted a 10-day canoe trip I did in the summer of 2015 in the Yukon Territory. You can view the map here.
